IPV Support Resources

A small collection of references on coercive control, complex post-traumatic stress, and how to recognize and respond to abuse

This collection exists for people who are trying to understand or respond to coercive control and intimate partner violence — survivors looking for vocabulary for their experience, family members and friends trying to support someone, pastors and lay counselors receiving disclosures, and skeptics who want to understand the research before forming an opinion.

Each document is written for a specific audience. Pick the one that fits the situation.

If you are in immediate danger
Call 911
National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233  •  Text START to 88788  •  24/7, confidential, free

For understanding coercive control

For anyone trying to understand the pattern — friends, family, community members, skeptics, clinicians, attorneys.

For everyone
The Coercive Control Playbook
Why someone can look like a perfectly lovely family in public and be something very different at home. Walks through the public/private split, why family and community consistently miss the pattern, the effect on children, and what to do when you've understood. Cites the academic and clinical literature.

For understanding the aftermath

A reference document on Complex PTSD — the condition that commonly develops after sustained coercive control.

For survivors and supporters
Complex PTSD: A Reference
What CPTSD is, how it develops, what it looks like, and what helps. Diagnostic criteria, prevalence, neurobiology, comorbidity, and treatment evidence with citations, written in readable prose.

For people receiving disclosures in faith communities

For anyone in a position to receive a disclosure of abuse — formally or informally — within a Christian community.

For lay helpers
When Someone Tells You Their Marriage Is Bad
For small group leaders, prayer partners, and friends in a church environment. Recognizing psychological abuse and coercive control, responding without making it worse, and knowing when to get out of the way. Includes printable reference cards.
For pastors and elders
When Marriage Becomes Captivity
A practical and scriptural guide for pastors, elders, and lay helpers. Recognizing coercive control, neurodivergent and immigrant-family dynamics, what scripture actually says about abuse and marriage, common pastoral mistakes, and practical protocols. Includes printable reference card.

Compiled April 2026. These documents summarize the established academic and clinical literature on coercive control, intimate partner violence, and complex post-traumatic stress. They are general references, not legal or medical advice, and do not substitute for evaluation by qualified professionals. Free to copy, distribute, and adapt.
